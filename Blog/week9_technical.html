My Thinking Style

According to Gregorc’s Mind Styles model, I’m an Abstract Random learner. Thinkers like me tend to organize information through reflection. We learn best when :

  • in a personalized environment
  • given broad or general guidelines
  • able to maintain friendly relationships
  • able to participate in group activities

I would agree that most of this suits me. Throughout most of my experience in educational environments from grade school to college to grad school—and now Dev Bootcamp— I know that I’m learning when I have time to reflect on the material. It’s my way of checking in with myself.

Some of the things Abstract Random thinkers struggle with are:

  • competition
  • working with authoritarian personalities
  • working in restrictive environments
  • working with people who seem unfriendly
  • concentrating on one thing at a time

In phase 0 of Dev Bootcamp, the biggest struggle for me has been concentrating on one thing at a time, mostly because I have the shortest attention span ever. I get distracted very easily and my mind tends to wander far, far away if I’m working on one thing for too long.

The most helpful thing for me going forward I think will be to take breaks when I need them. Not only does it help to break up a long session, but I also get to step back and do some reflecting on what I’ve been working on.
